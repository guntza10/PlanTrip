## Draft 1

Certainly! Fukuoka is a beautiful city located on the northern shore of the island of Kyushu in Japan. Here's a suggested 6-day itinerary to help you make the most of your time in Fukuoka:

Day 1: Arrival and Fukuoka Tower

Arrive in Fukuoka and check into your hotel.
Visit Fukuoka Tower, which offers stunning views of the city and the surrounding area.
Explore the nearby Momochi Seaside Park, a beautiful waterfront park with plenty of green space.
Day 2: Fukuoka City Museum and Canal City Hakata

Visit the Fukuoka City Museum, which houses exhibits on the history and culture of the city.
Head to Canal City Hakata, a large shopping and entertainment complex with a canal running through it. There are plenty of shops, restaurants, and entertainment options here to keep you busy for several hours.
Day 3: Tenjin and Ohori Park

Spend the day exploring the Tenjin area, which is Fukuoka's main commercial and entertainment district. There are plenty of shops, restaurants, and bars to check out here.
In the afternoon, head to Ohori Park, a large public park that's perfect for a relaxing stroll.
Day 4: Nanzoin Temple and Yanagawa Canal Boat Tour

Take a day trip to Nanzoin Temple, one of the largest temples in Japan. It's located in Sasaguri, about 30 minutes from Fukuoka by train.
In the afternoon, head to Yanagawa and take a canal boat tour. Yanagawa is known for its picturesque canals and is often referred to as "the Venice of Japan."
Day 5: Dazaifu and Kyushu National Museum

Take a day trip to Dazaifu, a small town about 30 minutes from Fukuoka by train. It's home to several important historical sites, including Dazaifu Tenmangu Shrine.
After visiting Dazaifu, head to the Kyushu National Museum, which has exhibits on the history and culture of the Kyushu region.
Day 6: Sumiyoshi Shrine and Departure

Visit Sumiyoshi Shrine, which is known for its unique architectural style and beautiful gardens.
Depart from Fukuoka.
I hope this itinerary gives you some ideas for your trip to Fukuoka. Of course, feel free to adjust it to suit your interests and preferences!
